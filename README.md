# pendeln 🚃

*pendeln (v.) — to commute. Also: what I'm doing every morning while Duolingo teaches me how to say "the owl drinks milk" for the 47th time.*

**pendeln** is a German learning app that learns from *my* German classes. I feed it my weekly lecture content, and it turns my commute into short, meaningful practice sessions that actually match what I'm studying — not some generic A1-to-C2 conveyor belt.

This is a personal portfolio project, built to be genuinely used (by me, daily, on the U-Bahn) and genuinely useful (as a case study in product thinking + AI-powered content pipelines).

## The one-liner

> Your German course, chopped into commute-sized lessons that adapt to what your professor taught this week.

## Why this exists

Every language app I've tried has the same three problems:

1. **It doesn't know where I am.** Placement tests are a joke — I'm B1 in reading, A2 in speaking, and somehow the app decides I need to learn colors again.
2. **It doesn't know what I'm learning.** My course covered Konjunktiv II last week. My app is teaching me fruit vocabulary. These two things never meet.
3. **It optimizes for streaks, not learning.** Gamification designed to keep me tapping, not to make Wednesday's class easier.

pendeln flips the model: the *course* is the curriculum, the app is the practice layer.
